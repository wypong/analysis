Corollary 7.3.2.

If \(f \in C[a,b]\text{,}\) then the indefinite integral
\begin{equation*} F(x)=\int_a^x f(t)\,dt \end{equation*}
is an antiderivative, or primitive, of \(f\) on \([a,b]\text{.}\)
🔗
🔗

Proof.
By Continuous Functions Are Integrable, every continuous function on \([a,b]\) is Riemann integrable. Therefore Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (Second Form) applies at every point of \([a,b]\) and yields \(F'=f\text{.}\)
