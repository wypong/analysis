Lemma 8.4.4.

Let \(h \in C^2(\R)\) satisfy
\begin{equation*} h''(x)=-h(x) \qquad \text{for all } x \in \R, \end{equation*}
together with \(h(0)=0\) and \(h'(0)=0\text{.}\) Then \(h(x)=0\) for every \(x \in \R\text{.}\)
🔗
🔗

Proof.

Consider the function
\begin{equation*} E(x)=h(x)^2+h'(x)^2. \end{equation*}
Differentiating gives
\begin{equation*} E'(x)=2h(x)h'(x)+2h'(x)h''(x) = 2h'(x)\bigl(h(x)+h''(x)\bigr) =0. \end{equation*}
Hence \(E\) is constant by Corollary 5.4.5. Since \(E(0)=h(0)^2+h'(0)^2=0\text{,}\) we have \(E(x)=0\) for every \(x\text{.}\) Therefore both \(h(x)\) and \(h'(x)\) are identically zero.
