Proposition 8.4.3.

For every \(x \in \R\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} \sin^2 x+\cos^2 x=1. \end{equation*}
🔗
🔗

Proof.
Let \(h(x)=\sin^2 x+\cos^2 x\text{.}\) Using the product rule and Proposition 8.4.2, we get
\begin{equation*} h'(x)=2\sin x \cos x + 2\cos x(-\sin x)=0. \end{equation*}
By Corollary 5.4.5, \(h\) is constant on \(\R\text{.}\) Evaluating at \(x=0\) gives
\begin{equation*} h(0)=\sin^2 0+\cos^2 0=0^2+1^2=1. \end{equation*}
Hence \(h(x)=1\) for all \(x\text{.}\)
