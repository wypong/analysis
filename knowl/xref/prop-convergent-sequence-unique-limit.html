Proposition 2.1.3.

A convergent sequence has a unique limit.
🔗
🔗

Proof.

Suppose \(x_n \to r\) and \(x_n \to r'\text{.}\) Then both \((r-x_n)\) and \((x_n-r')\) are null sequences. By Proposition 2.1.2, their sum \((r-r')\) is also null. A constant sequence is null only if its constant value is zero, so \(r-r'=0\text{.}\) Therefore \(r=r'\text{.}\)
