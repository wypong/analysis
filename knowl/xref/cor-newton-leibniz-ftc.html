Corollary 7.3.6.

Let \(f \colon [a,b] \to \R\) be continuous, and let \(F\) be an antiderivative of \(f\) on \([a,b]\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation*} \int_a^b f(x)\,dx = F(b)-F(a). \end{equation*}
🔗
🔗

Proof.
By Continuous Functions Are Integrable, the function \(f\) is Riemann integrable on \([a,b]\text{.}\) Since \(F'=f\text{,}\) the conclusion follows from Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (First Form).
