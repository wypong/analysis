Proposition 8.3.4.

The exponential function satisfies
\begin{equation*} \frac{d}{dx} e^x = e^x \qquad \text{for all } x \in \R. \end{equation*}
🔗
🔗

Proof.
The power series for \(e^x\) has radius of convergence \(\infty\text{,}\) so by Theorem 6.2.2 it may be differentiated term by term:
\begin{equation*} \frac{d}{dx} e^x = \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{n x^{n-1}}{n!} = \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{x^{n-1}}{(n-1)!} = \sum_{m=0}^\infty \frac{x^m}{m!} = e^x. \end{equation*}
