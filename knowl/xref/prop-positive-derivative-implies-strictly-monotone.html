Proposition 5.4.2.

Let \(I\) be an open interval, and let \(f \colon I \to \R\) be differentiable on \(I\text{.}\)
🔗
  1. If \(f'(x) \gt 0\) for every \(x \in I\text{,}\) then \(f\) is strictly increasing on \(I\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  2. If \(f'(x) \lt 0\) for every \(x \in I\text{,}\) then \(f\) is strictly decreasing on \(I\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
🔗

Proof.
Again we prove only the increasing case. Let \(x_1,x_2 \in I\) with \(x_1 \lt x_2\text{.}\) By the Mean Value Theorem (Theorem 5.3.3), there exists \(c \in (x_1,x_2)\) such that
\begin{equation*} f(x_2)-f(x_1)=f'(c)(x_2-x_1). \end{equation*}
Since \(f'(c) \gt 0\) and \(x_2-x_1 \gt 0\text{,}\) we get \(f(x_2)-f(x_1) \gt 0\text{.}\) Therefore \(f\) is strictly increasing on \(I\text{.}\)
