Proposition E.1.4.

The union of two null sets is null.
🔗
🔗

Proof.

Let \(A\) and \(B\) be null sets, and let \(\varepsilon \gt 0\text{.}\) Choose open intervals \((I_k)\) covering \(A\) with
\begin{equation*} \sum_{k=1}^{\infty}|I_k| \le \frac{\varepsilon}{2}, \end{equation*}
and open intervals \((J_k)\) covering \(B\) with
\begin{equation*} \sum_{k=1}^{\infty}|J_k| \le \frac{\varepsilon}{2}. \end{equation*}
Then the countable family consisting of all the intervals \(I_k\) and \(J_k\) covers \(A \cup B\text{,}\) and the total sum of their lengths is at most \(\varepsilon\text{.}\) Hence \(A \cup B\) is null.
