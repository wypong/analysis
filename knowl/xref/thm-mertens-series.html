Theorem 3.3.1. Mertens’ Theorem.

Suppose
\begin{equation*} \sum_{n=0}^\infty a_n = A \qquad \text{and} \qquad \sum_{n=0}^\infty b_n = B \end{equation*}
both converge, and suppose that at least one of these series converges absolutely. Then the Cauchy product
\begin{equation*} \sum_{n=0}^\infty c_n \end{equation*}
converges, and
\begin{equation*} \sum_{n=0}^\infty c_n = AB. \end{equation*}
