Corollary E.1.3.

Every countable subset of \(\R\) is a null set.
🔗
🔗

Proof.
Let \(A=\{a_1,a_2,\dots\}\) be countably infinite. Given \(\varepsilon \gt 0\text{,}\) put
\begin{equation*} I_k=\left(a_k-\frac{\varepsilon}{2^{k+1}}, a_k+\frac{\varepsilon}{2^{k+1}}\right). \end{equation*}
Then \(A \subseteq \bigcup_{k=1}^{\infty} I_k\) and
\begin{equation*} \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} |I_k| = \sum_{k=1}^{\infty}\frac{\varepsilon}{2^k} = \varepsilon. \end{equation*}
Finite sets are treated in the same way, by allowing all but finitely many intervals to be empty.
