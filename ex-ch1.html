    Exercises 1.3 Exercises

    1.

    Let \(A = \{1-1/n : n\in\N\}\text{.}\) Find all upper bounds of \(A\) and all lower bounds of \(A\text{.}\) Is \(A\) bounded?
    🔗
    🔗

    2.

    Give an example of bounded sets \(A_n\subseteq\R\) (\(n\in\N\)) such that \(\bigcup_{n=1}^{\infty}A_n\) is not bounded. Give an example of two non-empty sets bounded above whose intersection is empty.
    🔗
    🔗

    3.

    For each set, decide whether it is an interval. Justify your answer.
    🔗
    • \(\displaystyle \Q\cap(0,1)\)
      🔗
    • \(\displaystyle [0,1)\cup\{2\}\)
      🔗
    • \(\displaystyle \{x\in\R : x^2\lt 2\}\)
      🔗
    • \(\displaystyle \{x\in\R : x\le 3\}\)
      🔗
    🔗

    4.

    Suppose \(A\) is a nonempty subset of a bounded set \(B\subseteq\R\text{.}\) Show that
    \begin{equation*} \inf B \le \inf A \le \sup A \le \sup B. \end{equation*}
    🔗
    🔗

    5.

    Compute the intersection of the two intervals \(I = (-2,5]\) and \(J=[1,\infty)\text{,}\) and express your answer in interval notation.
    🔗
    🔗

    6.

    Give one example of two intervals whose union is an interval, and one example of two intervals whose union is not an interval.
    🔗
    🔗

    7.

    Let \(a\lt b\text{.}\) Define \(f:(a,b)\to(-1,1)\) by \(f(x)=\dfrac{x-c}{r}\text{,}\) where \(c=(a+b)/2\) and \(r=(b-a)/2\text{.}\) Show that \(f\) is a bijection.
    🔗
    🔗

    8.

    Is each of the following sets finite, countably infinite, or uncountable?
    🔗
    • \(\displaystyle \N\)
      🔗
    • \(\displaystyle (0,1)\cap\Q\)
      🔗
    • \(\displaystyle (0,1)\)
      🔗
    • \(\displaystyle \R\setminus\Q\)
      🔗
    🔗

    9.

    Prove that the least upper bound property is equivalent to: every non-empty subset of \(\R\) that is bounded below has an infimum.
    🔗
    🔗

    10.

    Prove that \(\N\) is not bounded above in \(\R\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    11.

    Let \(A=\{q\in\Q:q>0,\ q^2<2\}\text{.}\) Show that \(A\) is non-empty and bounded above in \(\Q\text{,}\) but has no supremum in \(\Q\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
